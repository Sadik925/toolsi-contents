Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Can I decrypt a SHA512 hash?

No, SHA512 is a one-way encryption algorithm, so it cannot be decrypted. Instead, it’s used for comparison and verification purposes.

Is SHA512 secure for storing passwords?

SHA512 is secure for hashing passwords but should be used with a salt or alongside hashing frameworks like bcrypt or Argon2 for better protection.

How can I verify a SHA512 hash using this tool?

Enter the original text and the existing SHA512 hash into the tool. It will compare the hash of your text with the given one and verify if they match.

Is SHA512 better than SHA256?

SHA512 offers a longer bit output and is more secure than SHA256 in theory, but it's also heavier in terms of performance. Choice depends on your specific use case.
