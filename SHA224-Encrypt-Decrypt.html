Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is SHA224 encryption?

SHA224 is a cryptographic hash function that produces a 224-bit hash. It's used for verifying data integrity and is part of the SHA-2 family.

Can SHA224 be decrypted?

No, SHA224 is a one-way hash function. It cannot be reversed or decrypted. You can only compare the hash with a known input to verify integrity.

Is SHA224 secure?

Yes, SHA224 is considered secure and less vulnerable to collision attacks than older algorithms like SHA1 or MD5, though SHA256 is more commonly used today.
